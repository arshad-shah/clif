# Code of Conduct

This is a small open-source project. The rules are simple.

## What we expect

- **Be civil.** Disagree with ideas, not people. Critique code, not
  authors. Assume the person on the other side of the keyboard is acting
  in good faith.
- **Be useful.** Issues, PRs, and discussions should move the project
  forward. "This is broken" is fine; "this is broken — here's a repro
  and what I tried" is better.
- **Be patient.** Maintainer time is finite and unpaid. Reviews and
  responses may take days.
- **Stay on topic.** Project channels are for the project. Unrelated
  arguments — political, personal, or otherwise — belong elsewhere.

## What we won't tolerate

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Slurs, sexual content, or hostility directed at someone's identity,
  background, or beliefs.
- Doxxing, deliberate misgendering, or sharing private communications
  without consent.
- Repeated bad-faith arguing, sealioning, or other forms of disruption
  after being asked to stop.
- Spam, off-topic promotion, or advertising in issues and PRs.

## Scope

These rules apply everywhere this project lives: GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, commit messages, code comments, and any other
channel where contributors interact about the project.

## Enforcement

If someone violates this code, contact the maintainer at
**conduct@arshadshah.com**. Reports stay private; we won't name the
reporter without permission.

Consequences scale with the behavior. A first incident usually means a
warning and a request to change course. Repeated or severe incidents
mean removal from the project — comment hidden, issue locked, account
blocked, contributor banned. We won't litigate every borderline case;
the maintainer's call is final.

## In short

Treat people the way you'd want to be treated by someone reading your
code at 2am trying to fix a production outage.
